Spoken English

"Spoken English" is one of the limited elective courses for applied English majors. It is a course that integrates theory and practice based on learning situations that connect language application and language service positions. One of the basic courses.

How to study this course? This course takes the scenes in study, life, work and social activities as the main content, and relies on various social problems, including vocabulary, sentence patterns, dialogue exercises in various situations, etc., to cultivate students' fluent English expression ability, Possess a certain awareness of cross-cultural communication, so that students have the ability to live independently in foreign affairs and English communication skills in all aspects, and lay a good foundation for students to engage in language application and language services after graduation.

Lesson Plan

1.Pronunciation

Pronunciation refers to the way we speak. Most people speak a dialect of standard English with accents that belong to the country they come from or live in. British English learners usually hear RP (received pronunciation), an accent often used in the BBC and other news media and in the course material of some language learners, but it is also very common to hear various regional English accents from around the world. common.

2.Intonation

Intonation describes the rise and fall of sounds in speech. The three main intonation patterns in English are: falling, rising, and falling and rising.

3.Politeness

Politeness is about keeping good relations with your listener or reader. There are two types of politeness:

showing the listener or reader that you value and respect them.

changing or softening what you say so as not to be too direct or forceful.
